How Agile Meeting Transcription Works

1

Enable Captions

Turn on live captions in your virtual meeting platform (Teams, Zoom, Google Meet) at the start of your agile ceremonies.

2

Select Caption Area

Use CaptiOCR to define the area on your screen where meeting captions appear, targeting just the text you want to capture.

3

Automatic Transcription

As your team discusses user stories, updates progress, or conducts retrospectives, CaptiOCR silently builds a complete meeting transcript.

4

Process & Share

When the meeting ends, name and save your transcript. Extract action items, create summaries, or share the complete documentation with your team.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I integrate with JIRA?

While CaptiOCR doesn't have direct JIRA integration, it creates plain text files that can be easily copied into JIRA tickets. Many teams use the transcripts to populate sprint notes, add context to user stories, or document acceptance criteria discussions.

Can it track velocity discussions?

Yes, CaptiOCR captures all velocity-related discussions during sprint planning and retrospectives. You can search the transcripts for specific story point estimates, capacity discussions, and velocity trend analyses to maintain accountability.

Is it useful for remote teams?

Absolutely! Remote and distributed agile teams benefit even more from CaptiOCR as it creates a shared, accurate record of meetings that team members can reference across time zones. This helps maintain alignment despite limited synchronous communication opportunities.

Can we track blockers over time?

CaptiOCR makes it easy to track recurring blockers. By documenting daily standups consistently, you can search across multiple meeting transcripts to identify patterns and persistent blockers that need escalation or process changes.

Does it help new team members?

New team members gain tremendous value from accessing historical meeting transcripts. They can quickly understand team dynamics, recurring issues, decision patterns, and project context without relying solely on team memory or sparse documentation.

What's the typical ROI for teams?

Agile teams typically report saving 3-5 hours per week on documentation tasks. Additionally, they see improved accountability, better implementation of retrospective actions, and more detailed information for stakeholders, leading to fewer miscommunications and rework cycles.

How does it help with ISO compliance?

For teams in regulated environments, CaptiOCR provides timestamped, consistent documentation of development decisions, requirement discussions, and process improvements. This creates an audit trail that helps satisfy ISO 9001, ISO 27001, and similar quality management requirements.

Does it work for all project types?

While designed with agile ceremonies in mind, CaptiOCR works equally well for waterfall project reviews, design thinking workshops, and hybrid methodologies. Any meeting with on-screen captions can be documented regardless of project management approach.

What about non-English languages?

CaptiOCR supports multiple languages including English, Italian, French, Spanish, German, and Portuguese. International or multilingual agile teams can document meetings in their preferred language as long as the caption system supports it.
